Help using DfE Sign-in

This is where you'll find guidance, tips and articles to help you use DfE Sign-in.

Reset or disable multi-factor authentication

If you are having problems accessing your device, you can reset or disable your authentication methods to sign in by:

  • resetting authentication methods permanently, for example if you have lost your device
  • temporarily disabling an authentication method for a single day, for example if you have left your device at home

I am a user

Send a reset request

To reset your multi-factor authentication, you need to fill out an online form which will be sent to approvers at your organisation.

You will need to:

  1. Click ‘I cannot access my device’ on the ‘choose authentication method’ page.
  2. Select 1 organisation to send the reset request to.
  3. Tell the approvers which service you are trying to use by selecting a service from the dropdown box.
  4. Provide additional information to support your request in the free text field.
  5. Review the details of your reset request before sending. Click ‘send request’.

An approver will review your request and can either approve or reject your request.

If approved:

  • you will be notified that an approver has responded to your request
  • your multi-factor authentication will be reset and all existing authentication methods will be removed from your account
  • you will need to setup a new authentication method the next time you sign in

Send a disable request

To disable your multi-factor authentication for 1 day, you need to fill out an online form which will be sent to approvers at your organisation.

You will need to:

  1. Click ‘I cannot access my device’ on the ‘choose authentication method’ page.
  2. Select 1 organisation to send the disable request to.
  3. Tell the approvers which service you are trying to use by selecting a service from the dropdown box.
  4. Provide additional information to support your request in the free text field.
  5. Review the details of your disable request before sending. Click ‘send request’.

An approver will review your request and can either approve or reject your request.

If approved:

  • your multi-factor authentication will be disabled for 1 day and you will not need to use a security code during this time
  • when you next sign in on a different day, you will need to use a security code
  • your saved authentication methods will not change

No approvers at your organisation

If there are no approvers at your organisation, you will need to contact DfE Sign-in to access your account.

Once resolved, your multi-factor authentication will either be reset or disabled.

If your request is rejected

If an approver rejects your request, you will need to contact DfE Sign-in. The support team will:

  • check your identity
  • review your request
  • support in resetting or disabling your multi-factor authentication

I am an approver

Send a reset or disable request

If you are an approver and want to reset or disable your multi-factor authentication, you will need to contact DfE Sign-in. Due to your permission level, you will need to raise a support request to reset or disable your authentication methods.

Approve a reset request

When a user requests for their multi-factor authentication to be reset or disabled, all approvers at their organisation will be sent an email to approve or reject the request.

To approve a reset request:

  1. Open the ‘DfE Sign-in reset multi-factor authentication request’ email and click the 'approve' button to go to the review request page. Or sign in through DfE Sign-in and click ‘requests’.
  2. Review the request details.
  3. Select 'approve reset request' and click 'confirm'. The user will receive a 'multi-factor authentication reset request approved' email.

Approve a disable request

To approve a disable request:

  1. Open the ‘DfE Sign-in disable multi-factor authentication request’ email and click the 'approve' button to go to the review request page. Or sign in through DfE Sign-in and click ‘requests’.
  2. Review the request details.
  3. Select 'approve disable request' and click 'confirm'. The user will receive a 'multi-factor authentication disable request approved' email.

Reject a reset request

To reject a reset request:

  1. Open the ‘DfE Sign-in reset multi-factor authentication request’ email and click the ‘reject’ button to go to the review request page. Or sign in through DfE Sign-in and click ‘requests’.
  2. Review the request details.
  3. Select ‘reject reset request’ and click ‘confirm’.
  4. Give a reason for rejection and click ‘confirm’. The user will receive a ‘multi-factor authentication reset request rejected’ email.

Reject a disable request

To reject a disable request:

  1. Open the ‘DfE Sign-in disable multi-factor authentication request’ email and click the ‘reject’ button to go to the review request page. Or sign in through DfE Sign-in and click ‘requests’.
  2. Review the request details.
  3. Select ‘reject disable request’ and click ‘confirm’.

Give a reason for rejection and click ‘confirm’. The user will receive a ‘multi-factor authentication disable request rejected’ email.
