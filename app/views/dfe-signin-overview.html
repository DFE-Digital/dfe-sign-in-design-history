What is DfE Sign-in

DfE Sign-in is a generic Identity and Access Management platform, its designed to facilitate Single Sign-On and fined grand access management rules for DfE services used by people in organisations and institutions (Such as schools, Local Authorities, etc...). We refer to this a B2B (Business to Business) and broadly speaking we cover all use cases where a user is using a DfE Service on behalf of an organisation that isn't the Department for Education itself.

What DfE Sign-in isn't

DfE Sign-In is not an Identity platform for DfE Employees working on DfE devices. Although some services may require DfE employees to sign-in through DfE Sign-In it is not intended to replace the integrated authentication provided by the enterprise Active Directory. (NOTE Federation with Active Directory is however, on our roadmap).

DfE Sign-In is not a replacement for GovUK Verify and should not be used as an identity solution for citizens (Unless a compelling reason exists) or for services that require levels of assurance.

DfE Sign-in Services

DfE Sign-in is a Single sign-on service which allows users to access several important services through one account, making it convenient and easier for users to access the services they need most.

In total there are 35 different services which are accessed and managed through DfE Sign-in.

Additional elements to DfE Sign-in

Manage Console

Manage console (also called DfE Manage) is a part of DfE Sign-in for internal DfE users only. It allows internal staff to Manage a service and it's users within DfE Sign-in.

Manage console allows users to:

  1. Configure a service
  2. Manage users
  3. Search for organisations
  4. Manage policies
  5. View service information

Manage console is maintained by the DSI team.

Support Console

Support console is a part of DfE Sign-in for DfE Sign-in support staff (also called BAU team or support desk) only. It is an admin console which allows the team to resolve and manage issues for users.

Pirean migration

Pirean (also known as IDAMS) is a legacy login service which several of the services now using DfE Sign-in previously used in the past.

The services which used Pirean (IDAMS) were required to be migrated from the legacy service to DfE Sign-in. Each service was migrated one at a time and added to DfE Sign-in as a usable service.

The DfE Sign-in team manages and facilitates the migration of each service from Pirean (IDAMS) to DfE Sign-in.
